Communication Networks

Public networks are networks operated by service providers, or carriers, as they are often called. In contrast to public networks, private networks are networks owned and operated by corporations for their internal use.

In many cases, the carrier owns the facilities(for example, fiber links) and equipment deployed inside the network. Fiber is deployed in many different ways today——buried underground, strung on overhead poles, and buried beside oil and gas pipelines and railroad tracks.

Local-area networks(LANs):networks within buildings spanning at most a few kilometers are called local-area networks(LANs).

Access:The access network extends from a central office out to individual businesses or homes (typically, groups of homes rather than individual homes at this time). The access network’s reach is typically a few kilometers, and it mostly collects traffic from customer locations into the carrier network.

Metropolitan-area networks(MANs):those that span a campus or metropolitan area, typically tens to a few hundred kilometers, are called metropolitan-area networks (MANs).

Long-haul:the long-haul network interconnects different cities or regions and spans hundreds to thousands of kilometers between central offices.
